Retail Sales & Storefront Performance Analysis

Sample dataset built for this project (fictional retail chain "LevelUp Games" + an online marketplace channel), analyzed with SQL and visualized in Tableau and Chart.js. Not real company data.

A simulated year of weekly sales across 6 storefronts and one online channel, 5 product categories, and store-level overhead. The goal: find where the business makes money, where it doesn't, and why.

Key findings

  • Online outperforms in-store on margin — 28.8% margin online vs. 5.2% in-store, despite in-store driving more than double the revenue.
  • San Jose is unprofitable — highest overhead-to-revenue ratio (34.4%) pushes that location to a net loss (−$7,868) despite average revenue.
  • Trade-Ins/Used Games lose money (−6.5% margin) while Video Games is the strongest category at 21.4% margin.
  • December is the peak month ($527K revenue) driven by holiday seasonality, roughly 45% above the yearly monthly average.

SQL: profit margin by store location

SELECT
    location,
    ROUND(SUM(units_sold * unit_price), 2) AS revenue,
    ROUND(SUM(units_sold * unit_cost) + SUM(overhead_allocated), 2) AS total_cost,
    ROUND(SUM(units_sold * unit_price)
          - (SUM(units_sold * unit_cost) + SUM(overhead_allocated)), 2) AS profit,
    ROUND(100.0 * (SUM(units_sold * unit_price)
          - (SUM(units_sold * unit_cost) + SUM(overhead_allocated)))
          / SUM(units_sold * unit_price), 1) AS margin_pct
FROM retail_sales
GROUP BY location
ORDER BY profit DESC;
